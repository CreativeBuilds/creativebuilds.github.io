The Resume

Q. What is a resume?
A. A resume is a tool to summarize your qualifications on why an employer should hire you.

Q. What do I put on it?
A. Besides your qualifications, you should be specific on your relevenace to the position you are trying to be hired for. Other information includes
  • Carrer Objective
    • Tells the reader where you would like to go with your carrer over time.
  • Professional Experince
    • If you've had any work experince with the job you are trying to go for, this is where you would put it. The more you put, that's on subject, the better.
  • Education
    • Where did you go to school? Do you have a high school or college education? What was your GPA?
  • Additional Skills
    • Anything you think would be relevant for the reader to know about you.

Q. What size font should I use?
A. Nothing to large, but no smaller than an 11 point font as it will start to strain the eyes.

Q. What font should I use?
A. The most common is Times New Roman, but you can use any type of 'Sans' or 'Arial' font. In otherwords it should be clean and easy to read, don't do anything special here.

The Cover Letter

Q. What is a Cover Letter?
A. A short letter or document sent with your resume to explain more about yourself and how you would fit the job.

Q. What do I put on it?
A. Introduce yourself, list the skills you have and experinces needed for the job, and if you're applying for a specific job now would be the time to mention it.

Q. Are cover letters needed?
A. They aren't 100 per-cent needed but employers value them and if you put in the extra effort it speaks more about you.

Q. What about recruiters, do they care?
A. Not as much as employers. Most have stopped asking for them, but if you do want to send one it's best to email, or mail it, directly to the recruiter.

Job Openings

Q. Where can I go to find a Job Opening?
    A.
  • The Company's Website
    • If you already have an idea f where to work, and they have a website, you can most likely find if they have any openings here.
  • LinkedIn
    • LinkedIn is a professional website that you can make a professional page about yourself and companies do the same. This makes it easier for employers and workers to find each other.
  • SimplyHired
    • SimplyHired is an aggregator. This means they will show ads that are paid for by employers or recruiters. They also look all over the internet for other ads to give you so that way you can do a search of what field you would like to go into and apply.
      the downside to this is they don't tend to remove old ads, so you may get an ad that was filled years ago.
  • Google
    • Just a simple Google search looking for jobs will come up with relavent jobs in your area that you can explore, although this option may require a little more digging on your part.

Interviews

Q. What should I wear.
A. It depends if the job you are going for is business or business casual. If it seems like more of a formal company then you should either buy or rent a nice suit, or a conservative dress if you are female.
If the business is casual, dress pants, dress shoes, and a button down shirt is okay for men. For women you can either wear a button down or a blouse with a skirt.

Q. What should I bring.
    A.
  • Questions
    • This isn't a one-sided interview. Make sure to interview the company to make sure its the right fit for you too!
  • Gum and or Breath Mints
    • You don't appreciate smelly breath so why even take the chance with an interview, take a mint before you go! Make sure you spit out any gum before the interview as it can be seen as unprofessional and distracting.
  • Resume
    • A backup of your resume is never a bad thing to bring to an interview and some employeers/recruiters may request one during an interview.
